  - Roster: pid-A (human owner) + pid-ci (owner role, host_role=ci).
  - Records exercised:

      b) generation-rotation signed by pid-A, cosigner = pid-ci's key
      c) genesis-migration signed by pid-A, cosigner = pid-ci's key

Submission:

Expected (all three):
  accepted[].length === 0
  rejected[0].reason matches /host_role|ci|audit|R5-S-04|eligib/i

Defense:
  Pre-hardening, three sites inlined the R5-S-04 host_role:ci check:
    - coordination-log.js::_checkRule5 (lines 829-834)
    - fold-rule-9b.js::_verifyCoSigner (lines 117-122)
    - fold-rule-9c.js::_verifyCoSigner (lines 105-110)
  Each spelled out `if (resolved.person.host_role === "ci") return ...`.
  Drift between sites was unbounded — a future commit could weaken the
  check at one site (e.g. allow host_role:ci on rotation but not
  migration) without the other two sites surfacing the divergence.

Post-hardening: all three sites route through
require("eligibility.js").isEligibleSigner(person, contextName). The
mapping is:
  - rule 5 (checkpoint): "owner-quorum"
  - rule 9b (rotation):  "owner-quorum"
  - rule 9c (migration): "migration"
Per eligibility.js::CI_FOREVER_INELIGIBLE_CONTEXTS, all three are in
the forever-ineligible set; any drift would need to be made in
eligibility.js itself, which is the single audit surface.
