F14 / HIGH-1 fixture — CI-host owner anchor MUST NOT become trust root.

Scenario:
  - Roster: pid-ci is role=owner, host_role=ci.
  - Accepted record: genesis-anchor signed by pid-ci's key (verified by
    rule-1 because the key IS in the roster).

Submission:
  require("posture-v2.js").resolveTrustRoot(acceptedRecords, roster)

Expected:
  Returns null. Per R5-S-04, host_role:ci is audit-only — NEVER eligible
  for trust-root binding even if the underlying record verified.

Defense:
  Pre-hardening, resolveTrustRoot (line 244-302 in posture-v2.js) built
  ownerVerifiedIds from every owner-role person's keys without checking
  host_role. A CI-host deploy key, present in the roster for audit log
  signing, could rebase the trust root just by signing an anchor record.

Post-hardening: the enumeration at line 250 skips persons whose
host_role === "ci" (the same filter enforced by eligibility.js for
every other R5-S-04 site: derive-n.js:86-90, fold-rule-9b.js:117-122,
fold-rule-9c.js:105-110).
