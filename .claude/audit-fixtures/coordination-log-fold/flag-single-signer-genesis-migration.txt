F14 / CRIT-1 fixture — single-signer genesis-migration MUST be rejected.

Scenario:
  - Roster: one owner (pid-A), host_role=human.
  - Record: genesis-migration signed by pid-A only, content.co_signers absent.
  - Fresh chain: seq=0, prev_hash=null.
  - gh_api_repo_owner_capture present, fresh, with capture_ts == record.ts.

Submission:
  require("coordination-log.js").foldLog([migration], roster, {})

Expected fold result:
  accepted[]: no genesis-migration record
  rejected[]: exactly one entry whose `record.type === "genesis-migration"`
  rejected[0].reason MUST match /co.sign|2-of-N|R6-S-04|degenerate|self-sign/i

Defense:
  Pre-hardening (before F14 commit fix(coordination-log)), the engine's
  module-default registry routed genesis-migration to a no-op predicate
  (line 464-479 in coordination-log.js). The migration walked past rules
  1-5 and landed as accepted: true. resolveTrustRoot then rebased the
  trust root to the migration's owner — a single roster member could
  unilaterally rotate the substrate's root of trust.

Post-hardening: registry binds "genesis-migration" → foldGenesisMigration
(fold-rule-9c.js), which enforces:
  - R6-S-04: 2-of-N owner-cosig, NO degenerate self-sign
  - fresh gh_api_repo_owner_capture matching new_repo_owner
  - monotonic to_genesis_generation > from_genesis_generation
