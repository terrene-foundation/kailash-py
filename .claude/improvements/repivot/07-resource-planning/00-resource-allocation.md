## Key Decisions

### Decision 1: Bootstrap or Raise?

**Bootstrap if:**
- You can work without salary for 12-18 months
- OK with slower growth (18-24 month timeline)
- Want to maintain control
- Have some revenue or savings

**Raise if:**
- Need team (can't do solo)
- Want to move fast (market window)
- Comfortable with dilution
- Strong network/track record

**Recommendation:** Bootstrap Phase 0-1, raise if traction

### Decision 2: Contractor or Employee First Hire?

**Contractor if:**
- Still validating (first 6 months)
- Variable workload
- Specific project-based work

**Employee if:**
- PMF validated
- Ongoing work needed
- Want knowledge retention

**Recommendation:** Contractors first (flexible), employees when PMF validated

### Decision 3: When to Go Full-Time?

**Go full-time when:**
- Prototype validates (Month 1)
- OR traction visible (Month 3-6)
- AND have 12-month runway

**Stay part-time if:**
- Prototype results mixed
- No traction yet
- Need income from other sources

**Recommendation:** Decide after prototype results

## Key Takeaways

**Resource planning is critical:**
- 1,707 hours total effort (realistic estimate)
- $100-350K budget depending on path
- 12-18 month timeline to full feature set

**Recommended approach:**
1. **Month 0:** Prototype yourself (80 hours, $0) → Validate
2. **Month 1-6:** You + contractor ($50K) → Build foundation
3. **Month 6:** Evaluate → Raise OR bootstrap OR pivot
4. **Month 7-18:** Scale based on traction

**Critical success factors:**
- Don't skip prototype (saves 800+ hours if thesis wrong)
- Budget conservatively (always takes longer than estimated)
- Validate before hiring team (expensive to undo)
- Generate revenue early (reduces dependency on funding)

**Acceptable outcomes:**
- Best: PMF achieved, scaling, $500K ARR (18 months)
- Good: Organic growth, profitable, stable (18 months)
- OK: Learning, iterating, small but loyal base (18 months)
- Acceptable: Pivot after 6 months (saved from full 18-month investment)
- Failure: Sunset after 3 months (wasted only 80 hours on prototype)

**Resource risk is managed through validation gates and flexible team structure.**
