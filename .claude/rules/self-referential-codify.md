# Self-Referential /codify Discipline

A `/codify` execution is **self-referential** when its proposal touches the loom-side artifacts that govern codification itself — commands like `/codify` / `/sync-from-*` / `/sync-to-use` / `/redteam`, the skills backing them, the rules governing artifact quality and trust posture, the hooks detecting violations, the bin scripts validating output. On those surfaces the agent is authoring rules that constrain its own next behavior, so a single-pass shipment can silently propagate a defect into every future session that loads the rule.

Per posture defaults (`.claude/skills/32-trust-posture/redteam-integration.md`), convergence (2 consecutive clean rounds) is posture-invariant, but the per-round audit DEPTH scales with posture — at L5*DELEGATED each round is mechanical-sweeps-only. On a non-self-referential surface that lighter per-round depth is the right trade-off — the blast radius is bounded by the artifact and the next session's deeper gate catches what a shallow round missed. On the self-referential surface that mechanical-only depth ships defects into the gate the next session would otherwise use to catch them. The forest-ledger arc (journal/0089–0098) cost 7 redteam rounds + a mid-arc Option A→B redesign before the substring-mask failure class was structurally closed — and per journal/0098 §FD #3: *"7 rounds of evidence say the single-pass L5 default would have shipped the R1 substring-mask silently."\_ (Pre-F119, that L5 default skipped Round 1 entirely; F119 made convergence posture-invariant, so the residual L5 gap is now the shallow per-round depth — which this rule closes below.)

This rule overrides the posture-scaled per-round DEPTH for that specific surface, and ONLY that surface — raising every round to the full multi-agent team regardless of posture — with a positive allowlist that names every file the gate covers.

## MUST Rules

### 1. Self-Referential /codify Carries A Redteam Gate Whose DEPTH Is Set By The Diff's Enforcement-Class (Regardless Of Posture)

When a `/codify` proposal touches ANY file matching the self-referential surface allowlist below, the mandated redteam DEPTH is set by the diff's **enforcement-class** — knowable a-priori, mechanically checkable — NOT by trust posture (already overridden) and NOT by the findings (circular). The enforcement surface stays at the full multi-agent team (Tier 1); a narrow prose-only, non-enforcement edit runs a single structural pass (Tier 2). The gate is depth-tiered; it is never SKIPPED for an allowlisted file.

**Tier 1 — ENFORCEMENT-BEARING → full multi-agent redteam-with-tests, UNCHANGED.** The diff touches a hook, a `.claude/bin/` script, `emit.mjs` / a validator, an audit fixture, or a management agent, OR adds/alters a `MUST` / `MUST NOT` / `BLOCKED`-corpus clause, a detector matcher, an allowlist entry, or a trust-posture trigger key. In this case the orchestrator MUST dispatch a multi-agent redteam-with-tests round before merging the codify, EVEN AT L5_DELEGATED posture, where the posture-default per-round depth would otherwise be mechanical-sweeps-only. The team MUST include at minimum: a code-review specialist (reviewer agent), a security specialist (security-reviewer), and a structural-validator specialist (cc-architect for artifact-quality surface, gold-standards-validator for naming/licensing surface, analyst for contract-design surface). The round runs in parallel — sequential single-agent review does NOT satisfy the multi-agent requirement.

The dispatched team MUST receive the proposal AND its receipt-journal entry AND the originating evidence (the journal entries the codify cites as motivation). Each team member reports findings independently; cross-agent disagreement on CRIT/HIGH MUST be resolved per `.claude/skills/32-trust-posture/redteam-integration.md` § Round-N — Cross-Agent CRIT/HIGH Disagreement Resolution (resolve by construction, never by averaging severities).

```markdown
# DO — /codify touches a self-referential surface file → multi-agent redteam runs in parallel before merge

Surface touched: .claude/rules/trust-posture.md (allowlist match)
Dispatched team (parallel): reviewer, security-reviewer, cc-architect, analyst
Each receives: proposal + receipt journal + originating evidence
Cross-agent verdicts reconciled by construction against the contract
Merge proceeds only when ≥0 genuine CRIT/HIGH across all four

# DO NOT — surface touched, posture L5, ship with mechanical-only per-round depth

Surface touched: .claude/rules/cc-artifacts.md (allowlist match)
Posture: L5_DELEGATED → per-round depth = mechanical-sweeps-only → skip the multi-agent team
Merge proceeds with no independent multi-agent review
(this is exactly the failure mode the rule blocks)
```

**BLOCKED rationalizations:**

- "Posture is L5, the per-round depth is mechanical-only — the self-ref surface needs no deeper review"
- "The codify is small (one bullet, one line), redteam is overkill"
- "The proposal only touches one allowlist file, the surface is narrow"
- "I am the orchestrator, I already reviewed the proposal as I drafted it"
- "The receipt journal documents the rationale, that satisfies independent review"
- "Multi-agent costs ~30–60 min of agent time; the codify ships value now"
- "Cross-agent dispatch was the prior session's rule — this session's posture overrides it"
- "Sequential review by one specialist after another is functionally equivalent"
- "The next session will catch it under its own gate"
- "If anything slips, the regression-within-grace mechanism downgrades us"
- "The rule's own bootstrap-circularity carve-out lets us skip"
  (the carve-out is one-time-per-rule per Rule 2 below, NOT a per-session shortcut)

**Why:** A defect in a self-referential artifact is a load-bearing defect for every session that loads it; the cost of letting one slip is N future sessions paying the substring-mask cost the forest-ledger arc paid. Multi-agent parallel review at one wall-clock unit (per `rules/agents.md` § Parallel Execution) is the structural defense against the failure mode the arc surfaced; sequential or single-agent review re-introduces the same blind spot.

**Tier 2 — PROSE-ONLY, NON-ENFORCEMENT → single cc-architect structural pass.** When the diff touches an allowlisted file but changes ONLY a `**Why:**` line, an Origin / length-rationale paragraph, a cross-reference, a typo, or example wording — with NO change to a `MUST` / `MUST NOT` / `BLOCKED`-corpus clause, NO detector matcher, NO validator, NO hook/`bin`/`emit.mjs`/fixture/management-agent edit, and NO allowlist or trust-posture-trigger-key delta — the mandated depth is a SINGLE cc-architect structural pass (cross-reference integrity + artifact-quality + no-dangling-refs), NOT the full multi-agent team. The gate still FIRES (an allowlisted-file edit is never un-reviewed); only its depth scales down to match the bounded blast-radius of a prose-only edit.

```markdown
# DO — Tier 2: a prose-only edit to an allowlisted rule → single cc-architect pass

Diff: reword a `**Why:**` line in trust-posture.md + fix one cross-reference. No MUST
delta, no detector, no allowlist change → Tier 2 → single cc-architect structural pass.

# DO NOT — run Tier 2 on an enforcement-bearing diff

Diff: adds a new BLOCKED-corpus phrase to security.md → that IS new load-bearing content
→ Tier 1 (full team), NOT a single cc-architect pass. (Tier 2 is prose-only, non-enforcement.)
```

**Fail-closed at the boundary (the existing tiebreaker, verbatim): any diff not _unambiguously_ prose-only defaults to Tier 1.** "Edge cases at the boundary resolve in favor of the gate firing" — where "the gate firing" here means the FULL-DEPTH (Tier 1) gate. The cost of a false-positive (a genuinely prose-only edit routed to Tier 1) is bounded to one extra parallel round — the bound the rule already accepts.

**BLOCKED rationalizations (Tier 2 misuse):** "the `**Why:**` reword also tightens the MUST a little — still call it prose-only" (a semantics change is Tier 1) / "I only touched one example, and it demonstrates a new BLOCKED pattern" (a new tripwire is load-bearing → Tier 1) / "the cross-reference now points at a new clause I also added" (the added clause is the enforcement edit → Tier 1) / "it's mostly prose with one tiny detector tweak" (any detector delta → Tier 1).

**Why:** The class the full team guards is _definitionally_ an enforcement-logic defect (a mis-fired detector, a bypassable MUST, a mis-scoped allowlist); a prose-only edit that touches no detector / validator / MUST-semantics / allowlist CANNOT introduce that class, so paying the full team on a `**Why:**` reword is the uniform-grind this two-tier split exists to remove. The entire enforcement surface stays Tier 1, and the fail-closed boundary means the split can only ever UNDER-spend on a genuinely inert edit, never under-review an enforcement change. This does NOT re-open the substring-mask class: that failure was an enforcement-logic defect, which is Tier 1 by construction.

### 3. Bootstrap-Circularity Carve-Out Is One-Time-Per-Rule

The codify that AUTHORS this rule (and any future codify that authors a similar meta-rule about codify discipline) IS itself a self-referential codify under Rule 1's gate. Running the gate on the codify that authors the gate is circular. Per `rules/trust-posture.md` MUST Rule 7 § Two-Phase Rollout: _"A meta-rule and its enforcement should never bootstrap in the same release — the rule is then drafted by an agent operating without it (mitigates red-team H4 bootstrapping circularity)."_

The carve-out: the FIRST codify that lands this rule (or a similar meta-rule about codify discipline) MAY ship as Phase-1 observer under the prevailing posture default (L5_DELEGATED's reduced per-round depth — mechanical-sweeps-only). The carve-out is one-time-per-rule and MUST be declared in the codify's receipt journal under a "Bootstrap-circularity disposition" section. Phase-2 enforcement (this rule's gate fires on every subsequent self-referential codify, regardless of posture) starts at the NEXT self-referential codify, NOT at the next session.

**Why:** Without the one-time-per-rule constraint, "bootstrap circularity" becomes a rubber stamp every self-referential codify cites to skip the gate. The carve-out exists for the genuine chicken-and-egg case (a meta-rule authoring itself); every subsequent invocation has the prior rule already in scope and IS the case the gate exists to govern.
