## Transfer Terms

### Unconditional Transfer

1. Patents transfer to Foundation upon Foundation formation
2. **No conditions** - No milestones, no performance requirements, no reversions
3. **No retained rights** - Terrene Foundation retains no special patent rights
4. **Complete assignment** - All rights, title, and interest transfer

### Why Unconditional

The founder is creating the Foundation. Conditions would be self-dealing—creating obligations to oneself. The transfer is unconditional because:

1. The Foundation will exist to serve the community
2. The patents exist to protect the community
3. Terrene Foundation benefits from community success, not patent control
4. Clean separation requires no strings attached

## Foundation Patent Policy

Upon receiving the patents, Foundation should implement:

### Automatic License Grant

Under Apache 2.0 Section 3, each Contributor grants a patent license covering claims that are necessarily infringed by their Contribution(s) alone or by combination of their Contribution(s) with the Work. The grant is perpetual, worldwide, non-exclusive, no-charge, royalty-free, and irrevocable (subject to the defensive termination clause). See the LICENSE file for the complete Section 3 terms.

**Note**: Once the Foundation receives the patents and becomes the Licensor of the Work, legal counsel should confirm that the Foundation's patent holdings are covered by the Section 3 grant in its capacity as Licensor/Contributor. The Apache 2.0 definition of "Contributor" includes the Licensor.

### Defensive-Only Assertion

Foundation may only assert patents:
- To defend users/contributors who are sued
- To defend the Foundation's assets
- Never offensively for revenue
- Never against community members

### No Patent Monetization

- Foundation does not license patents commercially
- No patent royalty streams
- No enforcement for revenue
- Patent value is community protection, not income

## What This Means Practically

### For Community Members

- Use the Apache 2.0 code freely
- Automatic patent license included
- No patent risk from Foundation
- Protected from patent trolls (Foundation can counter-sue)

### For Competitors

- Can use Apache 2.0 code (yes, even hyperscalers)
- Receive same patent license as everyone else
- Cannot be sued by Foundation for using the code
- Level playing field

### For Terrene Foundation

- No special patent rights after transfer
- Uses patent license like any community member
- Cannot assert patents against anyone
- Benefits from community protection like everyone else

## Legal Considerations

### Entity Name

The registered entity name is **"Terrene Foundation"** All repository files, patent office records (IPOS, USPTO), and assignment documents use this name consistently.

### Bankruptcy and Dissolution

Patent assignments are recorded transactions with the relevant patent offices. Once recorded:

- The assignment is a matter of public record
- It is not a contractual promise—it is a completed transfer of title
- The patents belong to Foundation, not Terrene Foundation's estate

**Recommendation**: Obtain Singapore legal counsel opinion on:
1. Whether recorded patent assignment can be challenged as fraudulent conveyance if Terrene Foundation subsequently becomes insolvent
2. Appropriate timing of assignment relative to Terrene Foundation's financial position
3. Any required disclosures to Terrene Foundation's creditors (if any)
