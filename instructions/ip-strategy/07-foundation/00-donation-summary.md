## The Clean Principle

Terrene Foundation ("Terrene Foundation") donates its foundational technology stack to OCEAN Foundation unconditionally. The donation is complete—no strings, no ongoing obligations, no special relationships.

**Why donate everything?**

1. **No self-interest questions possible** - The platform is truly open source. There is nothing to accuse Terrene Foundation of.
2. **Community development of the platform** - Foundation and community bear development, not Terrene Foundation alone.
3. **Ecosystem creates the market** - More builders using the stack creates demand for Terrene Foundation's commercial products.
4. **Credibility you cannot buy** - Genuine open source builds trust that fair-code licensing cannot.

## Transfer Terms

### Software Transfer

1. All donated software is relicensed to Apache 2.0 immediately
2. Foundation receives full ownership of the repositories
3. Terrene Foundation retains no special rights, governance positions, or veto powers
4. The donation is irrevocable

### Patent Transfer

1. Patents transfer to Foundation upon Foundation formation
2. Transfer is unconditional—no milestones, no conditions
3. Foundation will implement defensive-only patent policy
4. Under Apache 2.0 Section 3, each Contributor grants a patent license covering claims necessarily infringed by their Contribution(s) alone or by combination with the Work

### Stewardship

1. Stewardship of donated assets is a Foundation matter
2. Terrene Foundation may contribute as a community member
3. Terrene Foundation has no special stewardship rights or obligations
4. Foundation may appoint any qualified steward

## The Founder's Role

The founder of Terrene Foundation and the donated technology:

- May contribute to Foundation projects as a community member
- May participate in community governance processes
- Has no special governance rights in Foundation
- Has no control over Foundation resources or decisions
- May advocate for technical directions but has no veto

**The founder's voice carries weight because of expertise and contribution history, not because of any structural position.**

Upon receiving the repositories, Foundation should establish:

1. **Contributor License Agreement (CLA)** - Standard Apache-style CLA for contributors
2. **Code of Conduct** - Community standards for participation
