# Clean Separation: Foundation | Terrene Foundation | ASME

**Document**: 03 of 05
**Date**: 6 February 2026
**Purpose**: Define the clean separation between the three entities

---

## The Principle

Three entities. Three firing lanes. No overlap. No coordination. No conflicts.

Each entity pursues its own mandate independently. Success of one does not require involvement of another. Failure of one does not affect the others.

## How They Interact (Or Don't)

### Foundation ↔ Terrene Foundation

| Aspect | Relationship |
|--------|--------------|
| **Code contributions** | Terrene Foundation may contribute like any community member |
| **Roadmap influence** | Terrene Foundation may advocate like any community member |
| **Governance** | Terrene Foundation has no governance rights |
| **Funding** | Terrene Foundation provides no funding to Foundation |
| **Coordination** | None required or expected |
| **Conflicts** | None possible—interests don't overlap |

### Foundation ↔ ASME

| Aspect | Relationship |
|--------|--------------|
| **Training** | ASME may use Foundation training if they choose |
| **Standards** | ASME may adopt Foundation standards if they choose |
| **Advocacy** | Foundation does not advocate for ASME interests |
| **Funding** | No funding relationship |
| **Coordination** | None required |

### Terrene Foundation ↔ ASME

| Aspect | Relationship |
|--------|--------------|
| **Solutions** | ASME may hire Terrene Foundation if they choose |
| **Competition** | ASME may hire any builder, not just Terrene Foundation |
| **Pricing** | Commercial terms, no special relationship |
| **Coordination** | None required |

## Why This Matters

### No Self-Dealing

When Foundation, Terrene Foundation, and ASME are cleanly separated:
- Foundation cannot favor Terrene Foundation over other builders
- Terrene Foundation cannot claim special Foundation relationship
- ASME cannot be accused of sweetheart deals
- Everyone's motives are transparent

### No Conflict Management

When there are no overlapping interests:
- No ecosystem contribution negotiations
- No patent revenue splits
- No governance seats to allocate
- No coordination meetings required

### Independent Success

Each entity can succeed or fail independently:
- Foundation succeeds if community thrives
- Terrene Foundation succeeds if products sell
- ASME succeeds if members benefit
- None depends on the others

---

## The Founder's Position

The founder occupies a unique position: created all three relationships but controls none.

### In Foundation
- Community member with contribution history
- Voice carries weight through expertise
- No governance position
- No veto power
- No special access

### In Terrene Foundation
- Founder/owner of commercial entity
- Full commercial control
- Succeeds or fails on product merit
- No Foundation subsidy

### In ASME
- Council member (existing institutional role)
- Advocates for SME interests
- Does not control ASME decisions
- Does not direct ASME to Foundation or Terrene Foundation

---

## Test Questions

To verify clean separation, ask:

1. **Could Foundation succeed if Terrene Foundation failed?** Yes—Foundation is independently funded.
2. **Could Terrene Foundation succeed if Foundation failed?** Yes—products stand on their own merit.
3. **Does ASME need either entity?** No—they can use any open source tools, any builders.
4. **Can any entity veto another?** No—complete independence.
5. **Are there shared resources to allocate?** No—each has their own.
6. **Are there interests that need balancing?** No—interests don't overlap.

If any answer changes, the separation is compromised.
