# Contributor Intellectual Property Guide

## Who This Document Is For

This document is for anyone who contributes code, documentation, or other materials to the Kailash SDK repository. It explains the intellectual property implications of contributing.

---

## The Short Version

1. You **keep copyright** of your contributions.
2. Your contributions are **licensed under Apache 2.0** — the same genuine open-source license as the rest of the project.
3. The project is the **subject of patent applications** owned by Terrene Foundation (to be transferred to OCEAN Foundation upon its formation).
4. By contributing, you **grant Terrene Foundation a perpetual, non-exclusive license** to use your contribution as part of the project — standard practice for commercial open-source projects (similar to Apache Foundation, Eclipse Foundation, etc.).

## Detailed Explanation

### Your Copyright

When you submit code to the Kailash SDK, you retain the copyright to your contribution. Terrene Foundation does not claim ownership of your code. The project's copyright notice ("Copyright 2025 Terrene Foundation") applies to Terrene Foundation's own code; your contributions remain your intellectual property.

### License Grant

By submitting a contribution (via pull request, patch, or any other mechanism), you agree that:

- Your contribution will be distributed under the **Apache License, Version 2.0** — the same OSI-approved open-source license that governs the rest of the project.
- You grant Terrene Foundation a perpetual, worldwide, non-exclusive, royalty-free license to use, reproduce, modify, and distribute your contribution as part of the project.

This is standard practice for open-source projects with a commercial steward. It allows Terrene Foundation (and subsequently OCEAN Foundation) to distribute the project — including your contribution — under the project's license without needing to contact you for permission each time. The grant is non-exclusive, meaning you retain full rights to use your own code however you wish.

### Patent Awareness

The Kailash SDK is the subject of patent applications owned by Terrene Foundation:

- **PCT/SG2024/050503** — covering the platform architecture (Core SDK, DataFlow, Nexus)
- **P251088SG** — covering AI workflow orchestration (Kaizen)

Your contribution may become part of technology described in these patents. By contributing, you acknowledge the existence of these patent applications.

**Your rights as a user and contributor are governed by Apache 2.0 Section 3**, which provides an automatic patent grant. Under this grant, each Contributor licenses patent claims that are necessarily infringed by their Contribution(s) alone or by combination of their Contribution(s) with the Work. This is the same grant that applies to all users of the software.

**Important**: The Apache 2.0 patent grant does not blanket-license all claims in every patent a Contributor owns. It covers only those claims necessarily infringed by the Contribution as combined with the Work.

Contributing does **not** grant you additional patent rights beyond what Apache 2.0 Section 3 provides to all users.

### Originality Requirement

By contributing, you represent that:

1. The contribution is your **original work**, or you have the legal right to submit it.
2. You have the **authority** to grant the license described above.
3. The contribution does not **infringe** any third-party intellectual property rights.

If your contribution includes code from another project, you must ensure that the other project's license is compatible with Apache 2.0, and you must clearly identify the source.

## What This Means in Practice

### Scenario: You Fix a Bug

You find a bug in the DataFlow framework and submit a pull request with a fix. The fix is licensed under Apache 2.0. You retain copyright. You can still use your own fix in other projects (you own the copyright), but the version in the Kailash repository is governed by Apache 2.0.

### Scenario: You Add a New Node

You create a new node type and contribute it to the SDK. Same terms apply: you retain copyright, the contribution is licensed under Apache 2.0, and the Apache 2.0 Section 3 patent grant applies to your Contribution as combined with the Work.

### Scenario: You Write Documentation

Documentation contributions follow the same terms. You retain copyright; the contribution is licensed under Apache 2.0.

## CLA Context

The Contributor License Agreement (CLA) in CONTRIBUTING.md grants Terrene Foundation a perpetual, non-exclusive license to contributions "as part of the project." This is standard commercial open-source practice, used by organizations like the Apache Software Foundation, Eclipse Foundation, and many other major open-source projects. It ensures the project steward can continue to distribute the project without needing to re-negotiate rights for each contribution.

The CLA does **not**:
- Transfer copyright from you to Terrene Foundation
- Grant Terrene Foundation exclusive rights to your code
- Prevent you from using your own code in other projects
- Create any obligation beyond the contribution itself

## Contributor Checklist

Before submitting a contribution:

- [ ] I have the right to submit this contribution
- [ ] The contribution is my original work (or I have identified the source of any third-party code)
- [ ] I understand the contribution will be licensed under the Apache License, Version 2.0
- [ ] I acknowledge the existence of patent applications covering the project
- [ ] I have read the LICENSE and PATENTS files
