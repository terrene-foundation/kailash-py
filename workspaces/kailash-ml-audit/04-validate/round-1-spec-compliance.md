## Executive Summary

**Verdict: SPEC-TO-CODE COMPLIANCE FAIL.** Round 1 surfaces 4 CRITICAL, 9 HIGH, 2 MED spec-to-code divergences concentrated in three clusters:

1. **Tracking stack is bifurcated** — `specs/ml-tracking.md` describes a single `ExperimentTracker` with `log_metric`/`log_metrics`/`search()` returning `polars.DataFrame`, tenant-scoped keys `kailash_ml:v1:{tenant_id}:...`, and a long list of typed exceptions. Code ships TWO separate tracker stacks: `km.track()` → `ExperimentRun` (no `log_metric`) → `SQLiteTrackerBackend` at `~/.kailash_ml/ml.db`, while `MLDashboard` and the engine-layer `ExperimentTracker` use `sqlite:///kailash-ml.db`. The two stacks do not compose. Every one of the MLFP-dev known-real gaps is confirmed by grep.
2. **Spec promises that do not exist in code** — `diff_runs` / `RunDiff`, `TrackerMCPServer`, `import_mlflow`, `data_subject_ids` / `delete_data_subject`, `multi_tenant=True` strict mode, 9 of 12 typed exceptions, `sqlite+memory` alias, the `kailash_ml:v1:...` storage key shape, and RL lifecycle. Each is a separate HIGH or CRITICAL.
3. **Diagnostic adapter family is partial** — the `Diagnostic` Protocol, `DLDiagnostics`, `RAGDiagnostics`, `AlignmentDiagnostics`, `AgentDiagnostics`, `TraceExporter`, `LLMJudge`, `LLMDiagnostics`, `InterpretabilityDiagnostics` all exist and facade-import cleanly. BUT: `DLDiagnostics` has no `tracker=` kwarg (spec-implied, brief-mandated), and NO `RLDiagnostics` spec or adapter exists. These are separate HIGH findings.
